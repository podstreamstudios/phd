Innovation Between the Frames of an Experience Economy

An integrated article thesis examining how experience-intensive platforms govern the transfer of value between practitioner identity and market access — across the Sound, Food, Drink, and Light registers of live cultural production.

This page runs two simultaneous logics. Narrative logic — what Alexander Luria called Romantic Science — is the travelogue: a 30-year circumambulation in which each pass at the root problem arrives from a new angle. Portfolio logic — Classical Science — is the Trello board: five discrete chapter cards that can be examined in any order. Both logics are necessary. Neither is sufficient alone. The dissertation is the argument that they belong together.

The Root Problem — 30-Year Research Arc

THE PROBLEM OF REMIX: how does value move from the irreplaceable encounter — the secret recipe — to the reproducible copy that circulates across cultural systems? And who governs the line between the copy and the thing that cannot be copied, that is, its autopoietic identity.

First circumambulated in a 1995 New Venture Creation course at UBC Sauder (prediction market for indie bands as a proto-NFT governance mechanism). The three papers address this problem from three different focal lengths, with Chapter 5 showing the framework operating in an uncontrolled real-world context.

Research Logic · Classical Science

Portfolio Logic

The second logic running through this dissertation is what Alexander Luria called Classical Science: a catalogue of what exists, what has been built, what can be held and examined independently of the moment it was made. Each chapter card below is a Trello card — a discrete, portable artifact with its own tags, status, and relationships. The portfolio can be navigated in any order. The cards do not depend on each other to be legible. This is the logic of the tacklebox: every lure has its place; the collection can be inventoried; the inventory does not require the river.

In Wartofsky's terms, portfolio logic operates at the primary and secondary artifact levels — the tools and the recipes. Miller's Law (1956) governs the portfolio structure: seven chunks, plus or minus two, is the working memory constraint. Five chapters. Two meta-nodes (Narrative and Portfolio). Seven navbar items. The constraint is what makes the navigation count.

Ch. 1

Introduction · Methodology · 30-Year Circumambulation

Travelogue: An Autopoietic Return

Establishes the root problem — how to design systems that govern the transfer of value between cult and exhibition layers transparently and participatorily — and demonstrates it through a 30-year circumambulation. The travelogue form is not decoration: the form enacts what the content claims. As in Flynn (2006), the methodology is the argument.

The arc begins not at BCIT in 1999 but at UBC Commerce in 1992, where autopoiesis (Maturana and Varela) first entered the record. In 1995, a New Venture Creation course produced a prediction market for indie bands — a proto-NFT governance mechanism for the transfer of value between cult communities and exhibition markets. The root problem was present. The recognition was not.

Flynn (2003) Fisherman's Eyes — an IART 608 coursework paper applying Benjamin's cult/exhibition dialectic and Engeström's activity theory to Haig-Brown's Fisherman's Summer — is now identifiable as the origin document of the PhD's experience design research question, seventeen years before the Carleton application. Campbell River appears here not as biography but as geographic research anchor. The 2006 MASc formalized the discovery; the 2010 MBA translated it into business strategy; ten years of live performance (2014–2024) embodied it without naming it. The 2020 Carleton enrollment is George Crooney handing back to Doctor Obscurity.

Introduces S/E/E/D — Study / Explore / Evaluate / Design — as a Flynn (2010) refinement of Moggridge's (2007) non-linear design process model, mapped onto Wartofsky's three artifact levels (Study: primary / Explore: secondary / Evaluate: tertiary / Design: Engeström's double stimulation). Cole's (1996) coordinated lenses methodology provides the epistemological warrant: the lenses illuminate what each other cannot, which is precisely why they must remain coordinated rather than collapsed into a unified framework.

Ch. 2

Paper 1 · TIM Review · RPV Resources Layer · Sound Register

Miscategorized by Design: A Theoretical Correction to IDEO's DFV Framework

IDEO's Desirability / Feasibility / Viability framework correctly identifies three dimensions of innovation but miscategorizes all three. Using Christensen's RPV theory — reconceptualized as a coordinated system of analytical lenses (Flynn, 2010) and grounded in Beer's Viable System Model — this paper proposes the correct reordering and tests it against the Stratalab™ platform deployment in the Sound register.

Diagnostic case: Rockaoke Live! featuring The Naturals passed all three DFV tests and collapsed anyway. RPV diagnoses it correctly: a Resources-layer failure. The Naturals dissolved as a performing unit — the irreplaceable human practitioners whose cult value the platform concentrated in one group, rather than distributing across a stable community. DFV's Viability category is miscategorized as economic sustainability rather than as a Values question about what the system is organized to produce and protect.

The structural solution: Megaband™ distributes musician cult value across a registered community. Audiofish™ is the proposed digital platform with a fish.audio Siri-style agentic AI voice interface as the Mitacs architecture target. Norman (1988) affordance theory is the theoretical warrant for the interface design at the Tool Layer.

Ch. 3

Paper 2 · TIM Review · RPV Processes Layer · Food + Drink Registers

Variety Engineering in Hospitality Platforms: A Cybernetic Case Study of TableSet

Hospitality platform design in the Food and Drink registers is a variety engineering problem in Beer and Ashby's precise sense — at the RPV Processes layer, where resources (chefs, bartenders, kitchens, ingredients) are assumed present but the governance of the encounter between professional cult value and guest preference remains unsolved.

The exchange value app is the attenuation mechanism: it mediates between the chef's irreplaceable culinary identity and the guest's preferences, specifying pattern (which concept, which professional, which property) while delegating content to local professional decision. Two matched professionals bear distinct cult value: the chef (Food register) and the bartender (Drink register), whose cult value operates in two simultaneous modes — pairings (amplifying or counterpointing the food) and transitions (marking movement between course-worlds).

Narrative arc: From the Stratalab™ pivot — authorized by Anthony Bourdain as the tertiary artifact who names the structural homology between musician and chef cult value ("rock and roll chef" as a precise theoretical claim, not a metaphor) — into the TableSet platform.

Ch. 4

Paper 3 · TIM Review · Theoretical Synthesis · All Registers

The Autopoietic Mirror: Lenses, Layers, and Tertiary Artifacts

Five independently developed analytical frameworks are five lenses on the same three-lens autopoietic structure. The structural homology is not coincidental — it points toward something real about how human systems organize tools, practices, and meaning. The five lenses: Wartofsky's three-level artifact theory (1979); Engeström's expanded activity triangle (1987); Christensen/Flynn RPV (2004/2010); Beer/Ashby variety engineering (1956/1973); Benjamin/Flynn exchange value framework (1935/2006).

The paper develops three orderings of one structure (RPV diagnostic / PRV pedagogical / PSL governance), the ARIES certificate as encoded theory, the |F|D|S|L| schema with its internal vertical stack architecture, the AI commodification argument (Stage 2 commodifies tertiary artifacts; curatorial judgment is now the genuinely scarce capability), and the PSL three-and-only-three business models claim.

The Change Laboratory dimension: The dissertation itself is an instance of what it argues. Its named ideal outcome — stated explicitly in the Change Laboratory / formative intervention section — is the Don Norman Design Award (DNDA) Education category for the ARIES Certificate in Cultural Production and Platform Economics.

Ch. 5

Conclusion · Applied Ecosystem · All Layers · Uncontrolled Conditions

Return to the River: Location-Based Experiences in the Wild

Chapter 5 is not a summary of Papers 1–3. It is three things simultaneously: the empirical demonstration that the compound lens (|F|D|S|L| + RPV/Wartofsky/Benjamin, coordinated via Cole) works in uncontrolled real-world conditions; the MML future work proposal for the Light register empirical deployment; and the autopoietic return — the practitioner-researcher arriving back at the experience design question first posed in Flynn (2003) Fisherman's Eyes (Campbell River, Haig-Brown's river), having built the theoretical and empirical apparatus to answer it.

The field site is Campbell River, BC — five venues forming an interchangeable applied innovation ecosystem across the infrastructure spectrum: Campbell River Lodge / Goose and Gander (Lodge and Lab case study; primary Mitacs Application 1 site); Match Pub and Eatery at Chances Casino (primary LBE site; Evoke existing relationship; Audiofish Phase 3a deployment context); Painter's Lodge (heritage destination hospitality; Fyma.ai spatial analytics connection); Naturally Pacific Resort / Velocity Lounge (technology-forward premium comparator; Travel + Leisure 100 Best New Hotels 2025; Michelin Guide PLUS); and Spirit Square / Tidemark Theatre (showcase site; civic governance dimension; INFilm location library integration).

Each venue is a distinct pool in the Tyee Pool sense — a defined space with its own acoustic character, audience, and programming logic. The Tyee Club of B.C. has held its chinook salmon tournament in Campbell River since 1923 under a strict constraint set: rowboat or canoe only, 20-pound line maximum, single barbless hook. The constraint is what makes the catch count. The compound lens makes the differences between pools legible and actionable. The peer-reviewed papers are the membership pins — proof the catch was made under the rules.

The Light register is the empirical horizon. The Lodge and Lab case study demonstrates MML's three-layer AI previsualization pipeline — brand identity, physical space, and experiential context — applied to Campbell River Lodge / Goose and Gander, with River Lodge Whisky (Clearwater Spirits / VICSS) as the Drink register anchor. TheLounge™ (Mitacs Application 2) extends this into a live activation research program across the full five-venue ecosystem, with Multimodal Media Lab (MML) as primary industry partner, evoke.ca as visual design collaborator, and INFilm (Vancouver Island North Film Commission) as in-kind regional partner.

Chapter 5 closes with the autopoietic return. Flynn (2003) asked the experience design question in Campbell River through Haig-Brown and activity theory. Twenty-three years later, Chapter 5 answers it from the same geographic location — with the compound lens as the instrument and the Campbell River venue ecosystem as the field site. T.S. Eliot's Four Quartets (1942), cited in the Flynn (2006) MASc thesis, closes the arc: "And the end of all our exploring / Will be to arrive where we started / And know the place for the first time." The place is Campbell River. The return is the empirical deployment of what the 2003 paper asked about. The dissertation is complete.
